# MOH Integrated Shield Plan Comparison

Source: Ministry of Health Singapore, Integrated Shield Plans page

## IP Ward Tiers

Integrated Shield Plans (IPs) upgrade MediShield Life coverage to different ward classes:

| Ward Class | Hospital Type | Recommended For |
|---|---|---|
| B2/C | Public hospital, basic ward | Minimum acceptable coverage |
| B1 | Public hospital, 4-bed ward | **Recommended minimum for most residents** |
| A | Public hospital, 1-bed ward | Good value for those wanting more privacy |
| Private | Private hospital | Maximum flexibility, higher premiums |

## Recommended Minimum IP Tier

MOH data indicates that Class B1 is the recommended minimum tier for Integrated Shield Plans, providing adequate coverage at a reasonable premium while allowing use of Class B1 wards at restructured hospitals.

- Category: hospital
- Value type: plan_tier
- Recommended tier: B1
- Source page: comparison-table

**Key recommendation:** All Singapore Citizens and PRs should have at minimum a Class B1 IP rider on top of MediShield Life for adequate hospitalisation coverage.

## IP Rider

An IP rider covers the deductible and co-insurance amounts under your IP, reducing out-of-pocket costs. Standard riders cover 95% of deductible and co-insurance (5% co-payment retained by insured since Oct 2021 regulatory changes).

## Premiums

IP premiums vary by insurer, age, and ward class. Premiums can be paid using MediSave up to annual withdrawal limits. Excess premiums are paid in cash.

## For EP Holders and Foreigners

EP holders and work permit holders are not enrolled in MediShield Life. They should purchase a standalone private hospitalisation plan. Without coverage, a single hospitalisation episode can cost tens of thousands of dollars.
