# LIA Protection Gap Study 2022 — Key Findings

Source: Life Insurance Association Singapore, Protection Gap Study 2022 (published September 2023)

## Critical Illness Protection Gap

Singapore has a significant critical illness (CI) protection gap. The LIA recommends critical illness coverage of **3.9 to 5 times annual income** for working adults. The average Singaporean is underprotected against CI, which is the leading cause of financial distress in families.

- Recommended CI multiplier: 3.9–5x annual income
- Category: ci
- Value: 3.9
- Value high: 5.0
- Value type: income_multiplier
- Source page: 24

## Early Critical Illness

Early CI coverage pays at early detection and diagnosis, before conditions progress to late stage. LIA recommends early CI coverage of approximately **2 times annual income** as a supplement to standard CI coverage.

- Recommended early CI multiplier: ~2x annual income
- Category: early_ci
- Value: 2.0
- Value high: 2.5
- Value type: income_multiplier
- Source page: 26

## Age-Banded Protection Needs

The LIA study found that protection needs vary significantly by age group:

- Ages 18–35: Full benchmark multipliers apply (1.0x modifier)
- Ages 36–50: Full benchmark multipliers apply (1.0x modifier)
- Ages 51–65: Reduced needs as dependents become fewer and income horizon shorter (0.85x CI, 0.75x life)
- Ages 66+: Further reduced needs (0.70x CI, 0.50x life)

## Personal Accident

Personal accident insurance is recommended at **1–2 times annual income** for basic coverage. Higher for those in occupations with elevated physical risk.

- Recommended PA multiplier: 1–2x annual income
- Category: personal_accident
- Value: 1.0
- Value high: 2.0
- Value type: income_multiplier
- Source page: 30

## Underinsurance Statistics

- 7 in 10 Singaporeans are underinsured for critical illness
- Average CI coverage gap: SGD 255,000 per working adult
- Only 1 in 3 working adults has a disability income policy
